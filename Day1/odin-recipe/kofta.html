Malai-Kofta

Ingredients

  • Ingredients
  • 2 cups mashed boiled potatoes
  • 1 cup crumbled paneer (Indian cottage cheese)
  • 1/2 cup grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • 1 tsp green chilli paste
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala powder
  • 1/4 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • Oil for frying

    • Optional garnish

    • 2 tbsp cream
    • fresh basil
    • 1 tbsp chopped coriander leaves

Description

Kofta is a popular Indian dish made of spiced mashed vegetables, paneer (Indian cottage cheese) or meat balls that are usually deep-fried or baked and then simmered in a rich gravy. The vegetable kofta is a vegetarian variation of the dish and is a favorite among vegetarians in India.

Steps

  1. Boil 4 medium-sized potatoes until they are cooked well. Peel and mash them in a mixing bowl. Add 1 cup crumbled paneer, 1/2 cup grated carrots, 1/2 cup green peas, 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves, 1 tsp ginger paste, 1 tsp green chilli paste, 1/2 tsp cumin powder, 1/2 tsp garam masala powder, 1/4 tsp red chilli powder, 1/4 tsp turmeric powder and salt to taste.
  2. Mix all the ingredients together and knead them into a soft dough.Add 2 tbsp cornflour and knead the dough again until the cornflour is well incorporated.
  3. Divide the dough into small equal sized balls and shape them into smooth balls. Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the koftas in hot oil until they are golden brown in color.
  4. Remove the koftas with a slotted spoon and place them on a kitchen towel to absorb the excess oil.

Notes

  • *Make sure to mash the boiled potatoes well to ensure a smooth texture for the koftas.Don't forget to garnish the dish with cream and coriander leaves before serving.
  • Variations: Paneer Kofta: Instead of using vegetables, you can use paneer as the main ingredient for the koftas. Simply mix crumbled paneer with some mashed potatoes, spices and cornflour, and fry until golden brown. The paneer koftas can be added to a creamy tomato-based gravy.

Storage

  • Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.
